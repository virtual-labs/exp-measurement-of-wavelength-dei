**Step 1:** Set the width of the slit. Keep this value constant throughout the experiment.
<br><b>Given:</b> Slit width <i>d</i> = 1000 nm = 1 &times; 10<sup>&minus;6</sup> m

**Step 2:** Click on "Diffraction Pattern" or "Intensity Profile" option to observe the diffraction pattern as shown in Fig. 3.

**Step 3:** Change the value of angle of observation (&theta;) corresponding to the first-order minimum (<i>k</i> = 1).

**Step 4:** Repeat the process 5 times for different angles of observation. Take the average of the values of &lambda;.

<figure style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="images/procedure_fig2.png" alt="Diffraction Pattern of Single-Slit" style="width:100%;" />
  <figcaption style="text-align:center;"><strong>Fig. 3:</strong> Diffraction Pattern of Single-Slit</figcaption>
</figure>

**Step 5:** Calculate the wavelength (&lambda;) for each observation using the formula:

<p style="margin-left:2em; font-size:1.05em;">
  d &nbsp;sin&theta; = k&lambda;
  &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&rArr;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
  &lambda; = d &times; sin&theta; / k
</p>

Where:
<ul>
  <li><i>d</i> &mdash; width of the slit</li>
  <li><i>&theta;</i> &mdash; angle of observation at k<sup>th</sup> minimum</li>
  <li><i>k</i> &mdash; order of minima (k = 1 for first-order minimum)</li>
  <li><i>&lambda;</i> &mdash; wavelength to be calculated</li>
</ul>

**Observation Table:**

<i>Given: d = 1000 nm = 1 &times; 10<sup>&minus;6</sup> m, &nbsp; k = 1</i>

| Serial No. | Angle of Observation (&theta;) | sin&theta; | Wavelength &lambda; = d &times; sin&theta; / k |
|:----------:|:------------------------------:|:----------:|:----------------------------------------------:|
| 1 | 39.20° | 0.6320 | 632.0 nm |
| 2 | 39.30° | 0.6334 | 633.4 nm |
| 3 | 39.31° | 0.6336 | 633.6 nm |
| 4 | 39.25° | 0.6327 | 632.7 nm |
| 5 | 39.29° | 0.6333 | 633.3 nm |

**Average Wavelength:**

<p style="margin-left:2em;">
  &lambda;<sub>avg</sub> = (632.0 + 633.4 + 633.6 + 632.7 + 633.3) / 5
  <br>
  &lambda;<sub>avg</sub> = 3165.0 / 5
  <br>
  <b>&lambda;<sub>avg</sub> = 633.0 nm</b>
</p>

<b>Standard value of He&ndash;Ne laser wavelength = 632.8 nm</b><br>
<b>Percentage Error = |(632.8 &minus; 633.0) / 632.8| &times; 100 &asymp; 0.03%</b>
