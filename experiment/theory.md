
Light exhibits wave nature, and one important consequence of this is diffraction — the bending and spreading of light when it passes through a narrow aperture. When a monochromatic source such as a He–Ne laser passes through a single narrow slit, a characteristic diffraction pattern is formed on a screen.

The He–Ne laser emits nearly monochromatic red light of wavelength approximately <b>632.8 nm</b>, making it ideal for diffraction experiments because it produces sharp and well-defined patterns.

Let a parallel beam of monochromatic light of wavelength <b>&lambda;</b> be incident normally upon a narrow slit AB of width <b>d</b>, where it gets diffracted as shown in Fig. 1. If a lens L is placed in the path of the diffracted beam, a real image of the diffraction pattern is formed on the screen SS&prime; in the focal plane of the lens.

<b>Path Difference:</b>

<p style="margin-left:2em;">
  BD = AB sin&theta; = d sin&theta; &emsp;&emsp; &hellip; (1)
</p>

The corresponding phase difference:

<p style="margin-left:2em;">
  &phi; = (2&pi; / &lambda;) &times; path difference = (2&pi; / &lambda;) &times; d sin&theta; &emsp;&emsp; &hellip; (2)
</p>

Now, consider the width AB of the slit divided into <i>n</i> equal parts. Each part forms an elementary source. The amplitude of vibration at P<sub>0</sub> due to the wave from each part will be the same, and the phase difference of waves from any two consecutive parts is:

<p style="margin-left:2em;">
  e = (1/n) &times; (2&pi; / &lambda;) &times; d sin&theta;
</p>

Hence, the resultant amplitude at P<sub>0</sub> is given by:

<p style="margin-left:2em;">
  A = [ a &times; sin(ne/2) ] / sin(e/2) &emsp;&emsp; &hellip; (3)
</p>

Since the magnitude of intensity at any point in the focal plane of the lens is a function of <i>a</i> and <i>&theta;</i>, we obtain a series of maxima and minima.

<b>Condition for a maximum (approximation):</b>
<ul>
  <li>&theta; = 0&deg; &nbsp;or&nbsp; d sin&theta; &asymp; (k + &frac12;) &lambda;</li>
  <li><i>d</i> &mdash; width of slit</li>
  <li><i>&theta;</i> &mdash; angle of diffraction</li>
  <li><i>k</i> &mdash; order of the maximum (0, 1, 2, 3, &hellip;)</li>
  <li><i>&lambda;</i> &mdash; wavelength</li>
</ul>

<b>Condition for a minimum:</b>
<ul>
  <li>&theta; &gt; 0&deg;, &nbsp; d sin&theta; = k&lambda;</li>
  <li><i>d</i> &mdash; width of slit</li>
  <li><i>&theta;</i> &mdash; angle of diffraction</li>
  <li><i>k</i> &mdash; order of the minimum (1, 2, 3, &hellip;)</li>
  <li><i>&lambda;</i> &mdash; wavelength</li>
</ul>
